Projects:
Nevil
Originating from a joke with my friends, 'Nevil' became one of my first games made entirely with high-level programming. At the time of making 'Nevil', I was beginning my Computing A Level project and so decided to make it as my project instead of as a personal thing. Due to my Sixth Form focusing on Python, I decided to use the pygame library to make 'Nevil'. It's a game similar to 'Doodle Jump', the goal is to get the biggest score you can while climbing a randomly generated series of platforms that sometimes had boosts for health, score or jump height on them. In your way are multiple lasers, homing missiles and raining fireballs that damage you. After running out of health your score is submitted and stored if it's a new highscore. There are multiple additional options for 'Nevil' including background music, jump modifiers, new skins and volume settings. Along with a bunch of cheats that would mean I wouldn't need to be good at my own game :D

'Nevil' introduced me to more complex use of lists and arrays to handle infinitely generating structures. I learnt about the importance of getting rid of unuseful information in order to free up space for new relevant information and allowing for more optimised functions.
